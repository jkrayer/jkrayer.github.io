    Detail

    Astora (Temple of Axius)

    Astora is the arch-cleric of Axius, the goddess of the river Axius and patron goddess of the city.

    The “Baker‘s” Dozen once rescued her wayward daughter from river pirates.

    Philomelos (Temple of Hyperion)

    Philomelos is the arch-cleric of the temple of Hyperion, pillar of the east and Father of the sky. Hyperion is the father of the celestial family, Helios, Selene and Eos.

    House Kleidemos

    And ancient noble house with deep pockets known to sponsor Imperial Campaigns. Generations of this house serve the miliary, the faith, the government and rank in The Imperial College of Arcane Mysteries.

    House Rosencrans

    This family is currently living in exile as a result of the party's actions in chapter one.

    House Tantalas

    House Tantalas is slowly recovering from the scandal of chapter one. Their young patriarch, Alexandar, struggles to maintain the family's legacy.

    House Valencia

    Raymon Valencia has been building his trade network in a far more reserved manner since the loss of his eldest daughter, Katherine.

    Ovys the Oracular

    Sees a dire future for the empire and seeks ancient magic to keep Capitol safe.

    Sewer Rats

    Sewer Rats is the colloquial term for the Sanitation Guild. Led by chief engineer Phil "Philthy Animal" they're not above profiting off their unenviable position in the city.

    Temple of Axius

    The goddess and her river are responsible for the agricultural and trade success that fuels the Empire's expansion. Astora keeps the temple from politics making the temple's favor all the more coveted.

    Thefri Hercle

    An older gentleman with wax-curled moustache, crazed hair and rich foreign clothes who claims to be be a veteran and hunter. He is served by silent robed halflings.

    The Veiled

    This demon worshipping cult of gangsters was utterly decimated by the party's actions in chapter one. If any members remain they're keeping their survival quite secret.
