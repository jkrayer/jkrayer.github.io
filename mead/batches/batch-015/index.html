Batch #15: Neshanic Valley Maine Raspberry Blossom Hydromel

Part of Experimentation Matrix 2

Assumptions

Batches 7, 13 and 14 took approximately 18 days to fully ferment, which is far longer than I would like. Batch 6 fermented in 4 days which is amazing but it was at the cost of being undrinkable. So the goal is to find a reasonable middle ground where the sugars can be fully fermented in 4-7 days without the off flavors and smells of batch 6.

The mistake of batch 6 seems to be using the amount of yeast and nutrients appropriate to a 5 gallon batch in a 1 gallon batch; 5g yeast, 6.25g GoFerm and step feeding ~1.24g Fermaid O at start and 24 hours.

Batch 7 used just 5g of yeast while batches 13 and 14 used 1g of yeast, 1.25g of GoFerm and step fed .8 g of Fermaid O, 3 times and 2 times respectively. In each case the length of fermentation was approximately the same.

So it seems that the correct path to a faster fermentation is to creep back toward the amount of yeast and nutrient used in Batch 6 then monitor the result so I don't get to undrinkable. I will therefore try 2g yeast, 2.5g GoFerm and a step feed of .8g Fermaid O at starts, 24 hours and 1/3 sugar break. Reserving the right of course to adjust as I go in the event of bad smells or tastes.
