Batch #10: Neshanic Valley Florida Orange Blossom

Part of Experimentation Matrix 1

After making a couple of Hydromels (#6, #7) and finding them watery I've decided to try another traditional.

Assumptions

I don't have enough honey to cap out the yeast so I plan to let this batch go dry. I'm still resisting sulfates and sulfites and getting somewhat mixed results with cold crashing and pasteurization.

I plan to front load the nutrients as an experiment inspired by Hydromel: How to Make Session-strength Mead.

I've purchased a de-gasser and will use that for the first time with this batch. Current plan is to de-gas on days 1 and 2 then again before transferring to secondary.

"The Complete Guide to Making Mead" by Steve Platz, tells me Lalvin ICV D47 gives, "…ripe spicy aromas, with tropical and citrus notes…,"1 which sounds like a good match for orange blossom honey. Perhaps I'll leave this batch on the lees for a bit longer than usual.

Observations

September 17, 2025 After stirring in the pitcher I shook the carboy to add more oxygen which caused a lot of foam and overflowed the carboy so waiting for it to dissipate before pitching yeast. Because of this I will not front load nutrients since I would definitely overflow the carboy.

September 19, 2025 Massive overflow from light de-gassing so not pursuing de-gassing again until after primary fermentation. Will save more aggressive de-gassing for future batches where primary is in a bucket and therefore has enough head space for foaming must.
