Batch #6: Neshanic Valley Alfalfa Blossom

Part of Experimentation Matrix 2

A hydromel strength semi-sweet mead.

Assumptions

Based on previous experience I plan to start this batch at a starting gravity of 1.050 which should yield a 6.5% ABV Mead. I'll pasteurize the must at 1.000 then back-sweeten to 1.020.

Ingredients

  • 108 oz Poland Spring Water
  • 2 pounds Neshanic Valley Alfalfa Blossom Honey
    • 1.5 pounds for primary fermentation
    • 0.5 pounds for back-sweetening
  • 5 grams Lalvin ICV D47 Yeast
  • Go Ferm Rehydration nutrient
  • Fermaid O yeast nutrient

Observations

This batch has a "yeasty" odor that I find unpleasant and could have one of several causes, including too much yeast, excessive yeast nutrient added, yeast still in suspension due to pasteurization.

I learned from the "Man Made Mead" youtube channel that fermentation requires only about 1 gram of yeast per gallon of must. This in not exact since more sugar requires more yeast. Given this is a hydromel strength Mead 1 gram of yeast is likely sufficient.

What I'm smelling in the Mead may be yeast but it may also be unconsumed nutrients. It may be that I overfed the yeast which might not have required a lot of nutrient addition due to the lower sugar content of this must as compared with previous batches.

It may also be that pasteurization has left some yeast in suspension. The must is certainly cloudy which I believe (at this time) is due yeast in suspension.

Considering I still have about 3 pounds of Alfalfa Blossom honey I'd like to design a new batch that attempts to correct all of the above issues. This new batch would use 1 gram of yeast, 1 addition of Fermaid O and perhaps I would halt the fermentation with cold crashing. Not as positive about the last point since Batch #3 is also yeasty. I'm also wondering as I write this whether the amount of Go Ferm in the initial rehydration is part of the issue. If so I could also try a batch without nutrients. I'm not confident in my scale's ability to measure small amounts accurately so this could be a better alternative considering the tools currently at my disposal.
