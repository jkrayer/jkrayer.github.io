Batch #5: Neshanic Valley Blueberry Blossom

Part of Experimentation Matrix 1

A sack strength semi-sweet mead.

Assumptions

Based on Batch #3 and #4 I no longer feel safe predicting the rate at which fermentation will occur. However, I believe the rehydration nutrients and fermenting nutrients were insufficient in Batch #4 so I'll make an effort to correct that and measure whether that alters the rate of fermentation.

For this batch I'm targeting a starting gravity that should allow for 15% alcohol and yield a dry mead (f.g. ~1.006) that I'll back-sweeten to f.g. 1.020 for a semi-sweet Mead. I think this will restart fermentation and may require several rounds of back-sweetening.

Ingredients

  • 100 oz Poland Spring Water
  • 3 pounds Neshanic Valley Bamboo Blossom Honey
  • 5 grams Lalvin ICV D47 Yeast
  • Go Ferm Rehydration nutrient
  • Fermaid O yeast nutrient
