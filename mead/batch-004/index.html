Batch #4: Neshanic Valley Bamboo Blossom

Part of Experimentation Matrix 1

A sack strength semi-sweet mead.

Assumptions

I expect yeast will ferment at the same rate as in Batch #3. This will leave me with a dry Mead that will need to be back-sweetened. Back-sweetening may in turn re-activate fermentation. Several rounds of Back-sweetening may therefore be necessary for the yeast to die and the desired final gravity to be reached.

Observations

Fermaid O = 2.48 grams / Teaspoon. Go Ferm = 3.2 grams / Teaspoon. This is not exact since one can pack a Teaspoon, but Teaspoons will be good enough until a better scale is in budget.

Fermentation is slower than Batch #3. I'm attributing this to improper nutrients but could also have to do wth temperature. Next batch try to match rehydration temperature an improve nutrient accuracy.

Looks like fermentation is stalling @ 7/18 8:18 AM which means the Mead/Yeast may be falling short of my target final gravity rather than overshooting it as in Batch #3. Despite the gravity readings the batch is bubbling away so this observation may be premature. If true I'll need to learn how to kick start fermentation. Also it may be wise to lower the starting gravity of future batches since overshooting can be fixed with back-sweetening but I'm not sure how to fix undershooting.
